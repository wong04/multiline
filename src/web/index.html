How to play 🪐

Goal

Get your planets in a row before your opponent — across, down, or diagonally within one board, or as a diagonal spread across your timelines.

Your turn

Click an empty cell to drop your planet. Players alternate.

Forking timelines 🔀

Turn on Fork, then click an empty cell to spin off a new timeline — a copy of that board (both players' planets carry over as faded echoes). It opens a whole new board your opponent must also defend. You can fork up to the timeline limit of the ruleset.

Winning across timelines ✨

Imagine stacking your timelines and looking straight down through them. If your planets form a diagonal of 4 reading down the stack — each cell filled in some timeline, spread across two or more different boards — you win. The timelines can be in any order; what matters is the diagonal lines up across the stack.

Heads up: the diagonal-of-4 shortcut only counts when it's split across different timelines. Four in a single board is not enough — one board still needs a full line (5 in Full).

Why fork? 🤔

Because forks copy your planets as echoes, a diagonal you start in one board can be finished in another. Spread the four cells across your timelines and you complete a line that's impossible to make in any single board — and every fork is one more board your opponent has to defend at once.

Each planet can sit in a different timeline (numbered) — they only need to line up across the stack.

Rulesets

  • Classic — one board, 5 in a row. Just the basics, no forking.
  • Branching — 6×6, 4 in a board or a diagonal of 4 across different timelines, up to 3 timelines. Learn to fork.
  • Full — 10×10, 5 in a board or a diagonal of 4 across different timelines, up to 4 timelines. The real game.

Modes

🤖 vs Computer (easy/normal/hard) · 🛋 Hotseat (same device) · 🌍 Online (share a code) · 🛰 Quick-match (random opponent).
